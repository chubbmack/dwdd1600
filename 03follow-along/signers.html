Most Famous Signers

Thomas Jefferson
He was the principal author of the Declaration and one of the most prominent Founding Fathers. Later, he became the third President of the United States.
John Adams
A strong advocate for independence, Adams served as a leading voice in the Continental Congress and would later become the second President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
Franklin was an elder statesman, known for his diplomatic skills and his work in securing French support during the Revolutionary War. He was one of the most respected men of his time.
John Hancock
As President of the Continental Congress, Hancock's signature became iconic for its large and bold appearance. He is often remembered for his leadership in the early days of the Revolution.
Samuel Adams
A cousin of John Adams, Samuel was a strong proponent of independence and played a major role in organizing resistance to British rule, including the Boston Tea Party.
Richard Henry Lee
He is famous for proposing the resolution for independence in the Continental Congress, which led to the Declaration of Independence.
